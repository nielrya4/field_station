Our Facilities

Modern amenities in a spectacular natural setting

Main Barn

Our main facility houses essential amenities for field camp operations, including:

  • Fully equipped kitchen for group meal preparation
  • Spacious work room for specimen analysis
  • Computer loft with internet access
  • Three full bathrooms with hot showers
Outdoor Spaces

The field station sits on ten acres with stunning views and practical outdoor facilities:

  • RV hookups for extended field programs
  • Tent camping areas
  • Fire pit and outdoor gathering spaces
  • Direct access to the Big Lost River
Equipment & Tools

Professional-grade equipment supports field geology education:

  • GPS units and mapping tablets
  • Rock and mineral identification tools
  • Safety equipment for field work
  • Transportation fleet for field sites
Location Advantages

Strategically positioned for optimal field geology access:

  • Direct view of Borah Peak (Idaho's highest)
  • Access to diverse geological formations
  • Within an hour of multiple field sites
  • Remote setting with essential utilities
History & Support

Built in 2002 and donated to the ISU Geosciences Department in 2011 by Paul and Katie Link, the Lost River Field Station continues to serve as a premier facility for geological education. The station provides an ideal base for the renowned ISU Field Geology course, hosting students from universities across the nation in one of the country's most geologically diverse regions.
