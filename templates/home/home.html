The Field Station and the Fleet

Field Camp

LRFS is home to the ISU Geology Field Camp. Field Geology is a 6-credit capstone course taken by about 10 ISU B.S. Geology students and about 20 out-of-state students each year. The ISU Field Geology course is one of the best in the country, welcoming students from Harvard, Virginia Tech, Whitman, Pomona, Pacific Lutheran, BSU, WSU, UI, and USU.


Our Facility

Built on ten acres near Chilly Idaho, on the Big Lost River and directly in front of Borah Peak. The barn contains a work room, kitchen, three full bathrooms and computer loft. Built in 2002 and donated to the ISU Geosciences Department in 2011 by Paul and Katie Link.


Our Surroundings

During the 5-week field geology course, students work six days a week on mapping exercises in the Pioneer, Boulder, and Lost River Mountains near LRFS. This includes an incredible diversity of geology. Mapping areas include Darlington, Borah Peak, Carlson Lake, Boulder Creek and Copper Basin. Students create eight geologic maps and learn Geotechnology via mapping tablets and ARC GIS.
